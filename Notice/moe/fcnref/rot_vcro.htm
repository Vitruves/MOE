Rotation Functions: rot3d_vCross...

Syntax

w = rot3d_vCross [u,v]
a = rot3d_vDot [u,v]
norms = rot3d_vNorm v
V = rot3d_vNormalize v

Description

w = rot3d_vCross [u,v]

Returns the cross product of vectors u and v. u and v cannot be lists of vectors.

svl> rot3d_vCross [ [1,0,0], [0,1,0] ]
[0,0,1]

a = rot3d_vDot [u,v]

Returns the dot product of vectors u and v. u and v cannot be lists of vectors.

svl> rot3d_vDot [ [1,2,3], [1,10,100] ]
321

norms = rot3d_vNorm v

Returns the L2 norm of vector v. The norms of several vectors can be found by supplying them to rot3d_vNorm as vectors of first, second, third, and so forth elements. For instance, the vectors [a1,a2,a3] and [b1,b2,b3] would be given to the function as [ [a1,b1], [a2,b2], [a3,b3] ].

svl> rot3d_vNorm [ [1,3], [2,4], [2,0] ]
[3,5]

V = rot3d_vNormalize v

Returns vector v normalized. v cannot be a list of vectors.

svl> rot3d_vNormalize [2,1,2]
[0.666667,0.333333,0.666667]
