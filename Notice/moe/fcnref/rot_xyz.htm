Rotation Functions: rot3d_Rotation...

Syntax

    M = rot3d_Rotation [angle, axis]
    M = rot3d_XRotation angle
    M = rot3d_YRotation angle
    M = rot3d_ZRotation angle

    M = rot3d_PlaneRotation [angle, u, v]

Description

M = rot3d_Rotation [angle, axis]

Returns a matrix for performing 3D rotation counterclockwise (right-hand rule) about a given axis (through the origin) by a given angle. angle is in radians and must be a scalar. axis is a 3-vector; the axis is defined by the line through the given 3-vector and the origin. The returned matrix M is given as a vector of row-vectors; i.e. the matrix:

is returned as:

      [ [a1,a2,a3], [b1,b2,b3], [c1,c2,c3] ]

To perform rotations at arbitrary locations in space, the matrix transformation must be preceded by a displacement to the origin, then followed by a displacement back. If axis is given by , first subtract from the coordinates of every point to be rotated, rotate the points, then add back.

M = rot3d_XRotation angle
M = rot3d_YRotation angle
M = rot3d_ZRotation angle

rot3d_XRotation returns a matrix for rotating about the x-axis; it is equivalent to:

    rot3d_Rotation [ angle, [1,0,0] ]

Similarly, rot3d_YRotation and rot3d_ZRotation return matrices for rotation about the y- and z-axes, respectively.

M = rot3d_PlaneRotation [angle, u, v]

Returns a rotation matrix for performing 3D rotation counterclockwise (right-hand rule) about the normal of the plane defined by two in-plane 3-vectors u and v. The normal is constructed from the cross product uxv. angle is expected in radians and must be a scalar.

As the rotation is performed at the origin, rotations at a particular point in space must be preceded by a displacement to the origin and followed by another to restore the initial position. The returned matrix M is given as a vector of row-vectors.
