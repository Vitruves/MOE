Dihedral Contour Plot

The Dihedral Contour Plot is an interactive means to explore the energy landscape of two rotable bonds in a molecule. To open the plot use MOE | Compute | Conformations | Dihedral Contour Plot.

The user has to define two dihedrals in the molecule to analyze. This can be done by either selecting to rotatable bonds in the molecule before opening the plot, or if no bonds are selected, a prompt will ask the user to pick two sets of four atoms defining the dihedrals. Once selected the currently explored dihedrals and their values are shown in the MOE window with meters.

The application will then calculate the energies of the molecule for all possible combinations of the two dihedral values. The molecule fragments are rotated around the specified dihedrals but otherwise kept rigid. The resulting energy matrix is shown as contour plot with the two axes representing the dihedral angles from -180 to 180 degrees. Blue colors indicate low energies (i.e. favorable dihedral angle combinations), and the high energy regions are red indicating possible clashes. The current dihedral angles are marked in the plot with a white cross.

Left clicking in the plot will update the dihedral angles of the molecule to the picked value combination, modifying the molecule in the MOE window.

The panel controls are described as follows:

Dihedrals Change the atoms defining the two dihedrals for which energies will be calculated and displayed in the contour plot. This makes it possible to look at two other dihedrals without needing to restart the Dihedral Contour Plot application.

A prompt will be shown at the top of the MOE window to select the two sets of 4 dihedral atoms. After the atoms are picked the dihedral contour plot is recalculated.
Update Recalculate the dihedral energies and recreate the contour plot. This is useful, e.g. when one changes the dihedrals by clicking in the plot and then minimizes the molecule.
Export Export the contour plot in a variety of image file formats.
Attributes Open the Plot attributes panel, in which all plot settings can be adjusted, e.g. changing axis parameters or inverting the background color, etc.
